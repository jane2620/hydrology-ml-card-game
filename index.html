How the site works:

Users can generate a training and testing set of hydrology playing cards (as seen below). The goal is to study the training cards, and use these patterns to make predictions about the testing cards.

After inputting the desired number of cards and clicking 'Submit', you will be brought to a new page with a 'Training Set', 'Testing Set', and 'Testing Set Key', so don't scroll down too quickly!

To the left is an example training card.

Each card represents a site with a certain set of characteristics, represented by our input variables. We want to predict the water table depth at these sites using patterns we learn from our training data.

Training cards have four input variables, as outlined in the table below, and one output variable, water table depth.

Input variables can either be 'Low', 'Medium', or 'High'. For example, a 'Low' value for Total Annual Precipitation would indicate that this site does not experience a lot of rainfall each year.

The output variable, water table depth, can only be 'Low' or 'High'. For our testing cards, the output variable will be blank so you can make your predictions!



Term Definition
Soil Hydraulic Conductivity (k) Measure of how quickly water flows through the soil in a given area, in cm/min. It is important for understanding and estimating water flow through ecosystems and drinking water supplies.
Total Annual Precipitaiton Total rainfall in an area for an entire year, measured in cm.
Average Annual Temperature Average temperature in an area over an entire year, measured in degrees Celsius. Temperature affects evaporation and transpiration, which in turn affects water levels.
Ground slope Slope or steepness of an area. This can affect runoff levels, and therefore how much water infiltrates the soil.
Water Table Depth Distance from the surface of the soil to the upper surface of the zone of saturation, where the spaces between soil particles are completely filled with water. It is an important measurement in understanding groundwater levels.
